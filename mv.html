    FAQs

    Is Grow+Thrive vegan?

    Grow+Thrive doesn’t contain any animal ingredients in the final product. However, we’re all about transparency, so here’s the scoop: the natural raspberry flavour is made using ethanol that was originally derived from a cheese byproduct. That ethanol is completely removed during the manufacturing process and isn't present in the final vitamin.

    If your family sticks to a strict vegan lifestyle that avoids any animal byproducts anywhere in the supply chain, Grow+Thrive might not be the right fit just yet. We're actively working with our manufacturer to find a fully plant-based alternative so this one can be fully vegan in future.

    What does Grow+Thrive support?

    It’s a daily boost of 18 essential nutrients and 9 fruits and veggies—supporting immunity, growth, energy, and brain function. Basically, a high-five for your kid’s whole body.

    How is Grow+Thrive sweetened?

    Naturally—with monk fruit, thaumatin, and natural raspberry flavour. No added sugar. No funny business.

    How many tablets does my child need?

    Just one a day keeps the wellness rolling—for most kids, that’s all it takes.

  • Ages 2–8: 1 chewable tablet
  • Ages 9–12: 2 chewable tablets
  • Give it with meals for best results, and always supervise young kids while chewing. Psst: vitamins aren’t candy—don’t swallow whole!

    • Every child is different—if you have specific health concerns or questions about dosage, check in with your healthcare provider.

    Is Grow+Thrive suitable for daily, year-round use? 

    Grow+Thrive is formulated for ongoing daily use to support kids’ health and wellbeing across the seasons—whether they’re navigating school days, growth spurts, or everyday adventures. As always, check with your healthcare professional if you have specific concerns.

    Can Grow+Thrive help picky eaters?

    Grow+Thrive can help support the nutritional needs of kids who may not get enough variety in their diet. It delivers a broad range of essential vitamins and minerals, making it a helpful addition to your child’s daily routine. While it’s not a replacement for a balanced diet, it can play a supporting role in their overall wellbeing. For tailored advice, speak with your healthcare provider.
